Sonnets

Sonnets are a structured 14-line form of poetry that emerged during the Renaissance, traditionally written in iambic pentameter to create a rhythmic flow. This discussion will examine their defining features, including rhyme scheme, the octet and sestet, thematic development, the volta, poetic devices, and the distinctions between Petrarchan and Shakespearean sonnets.
